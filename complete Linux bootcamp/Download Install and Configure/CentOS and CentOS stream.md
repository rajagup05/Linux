
## CentOS and CentOS stream

CentOS Linux is the traditional, discontinued clone of Red Hat Enterprise Linux (RHEL). CentOS Stream is an active rolling-preview release that serves as the upstream development branch for RHEL.

### key deifferences: 

- **Release Type**: CentOS Linux was a downstream rebuild (released after RHEL). CentOS Stream is an upstream preview (code lands here before making it into the next RHEL minor version).
- **Updates**: CentOS Linux released major packages on a slower, stable schedule. CentOS Stream is a continuous-update distribution where packages are pushed regularly.
- **Lifecycle**: CentOS Linux followed a 10-year support lifecycle. CentOS Stream has a 5-year lifecycle based on RHEL maintenance phases.
